About

With a focus on supporting place-based adaptation across the UK and Ireland, the framework co-developed through the EPA-funded Transboundary Adaptation Learning Exchange (TalX) project will provide a roadmap to:

  1. Enable people from organisations/communities/sectors, both public and private, to work together to develop and implement adaptation to the impacts of climate change within a ‘place’;
  2. Provide a framework that enables places to assess their level of maturity in terms of developing the capacities required to support effective place-based adaptation partnerships;
  3. Support partnerships to progress adaptation by defining a process of change and providing practical actions to enhance adaptive capacity and deliver ‘on the ground’ activity.

What makes a quality partnership?
The TalX framework focuses on four key priority capabilities (Leadership, Evidence, Partnership, Resource). For each capability three stages of maturity were identified (Stage 1, 2, 3), from basic to advanced. and actions to support adaptation partnerships to advance through each stage of maturity provided.

For each capability there are three core elements that are representative of that capability:

  • • Leadership - Vision/Empowerment/Influence
  • • Evidence - Co-developing & maintaining an evidence base/Understanding & Communicating the evidence/Monitoring
  • • Partnership - Create a Healthy Culture/Build Strong Governance & Supporting Structures/Collaborate
  • • Resource - People/Assets & Communication/Funding
This research is part of the Transboundary Adaptation Learning Exchange (TalX) Project and was delivered by partners in University College Cork, (under the MaREI Centre), SNIFFER and Climate Northern Ireland.
